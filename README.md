## Overview

This project introduces **Shinto**, the indigenous spiritual tradition
of Japan, and the concept of **kami**.

Shinto focuses on harmony with nature and respect for the spiritual
presence believed to exist in natural elements, places, and ancestors.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## What Are Kami?

In Shinto belief, **kami** are spiritual forces or beings that exist
throughout the world.

They may be connected with:

-   🌿 Nature (trees, mountains, rivers)
-   🌤 Natural phenomena (wind, rain, sun)
-   👴 Ancestors
-   🏯 Sacred places

Kami are not exactly the same as gods in Western religions. Instead,
they represent the **spiritual essence within the world around us**.

## Example Famous Kami

Some well‑known kami include:

-   **Amaterasu** -- kami of the sun
-   **Inari** -- kami associated with rice, prosperity, and fox spirits
-   **Susanoo** -- kami of storms and the sea
